Frequently Asked Questions

Answers about freelance video editing for brands, documentaries, and content creators

How do you approach storytelling in documentaries?

I think of documentary editing like arranging photos in a family album. You want to tell a story that makes sense and touches people. I look for the heart of your footage - the moments that make viewers feel something real. Then I build around those moments, creating a flow that keeps people watching and helps them understand your message.

What's the difference between short and long documentary editing?

Short documentaries are like telling a quick, powerful story to someone in an elevator - you have to grab attention immediately and make every second count. Long documentaries are more like having a full conversation over coffee - you can explore details, build emotional connections, and take viewers on a deeper journey. Both need to keep people engaged, just in different ways.

How do you handle large amounts of footage?

I organize everything carefully, like sorting through a big box of family memories to find the best stories. First, I watch all your footage to understand what you have. Then I group similar moments together, find the strongest parts, and build from there. This helps me create a clear, focused story from all your material.

What makes a documentary work well on YouTube?

YouTube viewers decide very quickly whether to keep watching. I focus on starting strong with something that makes people curious. Then I keep a steady rhythm - like a good song that doesn't have boring parts. I make sure the sound is clear, the visuals are interesting, and the story moves forward in a way that feels natural but keeps attention.

How do you work with clients during the editing process?

I believe this is a partnership. After I understand your vision, I'll share early versions so we can make sure we're heading in the right direction. Your feedback is essential because you know your story best. I'll explain my choices and listen carefully to your thoughts. We work together until the film feels exactly right to you.

What if I need changes to the edited film?

I include two rounds of changes in my process because I want you to be completely happy with the final film. Think of it like trying on clothes - sometimes you need small adjustments to get the perfect fit. I welcome your feedback and will work with you until the film feels exactly right.
